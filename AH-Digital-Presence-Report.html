05 — Technical Findings

How the Website Is Built Is Working Against It

A technical review of adrianahoyos.com identified several structural issues that reduce how well Google can read, understand, and rank the site. These findings are presented below in plain language with the business impact of each.

  • The homepage sends 28 competing signals to Google instead of one

    Every webpage should have one primary headline, called an H1, that tells Google clearly what the page is about. The Adriana Hoyos homepage has 28. When Google sees 28 competing signals, it cannot determine what the page is really about, which weakens the page's ability to rank for anything.

    Technical detail: 28 H1 tags found on the homepage, confirmed via JavaScript audit. Best practice is one per page.

  • Google cannot read the showroom locations in a structured way

    There is a standard code format that websites use to tell Google exactly where a physical location is, including its address, phone number, hours, and type of business. Adriana Hoyos does not use this format anywhere on the site. As a result, Google treats the showroom information as regular text rather than business data, which makes it much less likely to appear in local search results and Google Maps.

    Technical detail: No LocalBusiness, Organization, or Product schema markup found on any page reviewed.

  • Dozens of pages compete against each other for the same searches

    When multiple pages on a website all try to rank for the same keyword, they split Google's attention instead of concentrating it. For example, the keyword "adriana hoyos design studio" has more than 20 different pages on the site all competing for it, at positions ranging from 2 to 75. Rather than one strong page ranking well, many weak pages cancel each other out.

    Technical detail: Keyword cannibalization confirmed across multiple terms. "Adriana hoyos dining chairs" competes across 47 separate URLs.

  • Search result previews are auto-generated, not crafted

    When adrianahoyos.com appears in Google search results, the text that appears below the page title (the description snippet) is pulled automatically by Google because no custom description has been set. This means the brand has no control over its first impression in search results, and the generic text reduces the likelihood that someone will click through.

    Technical detail: Meta descriptions missing on all reviewed pages including the homepage, locations page, and product category pages.

  • No individual pages exist for US showroom locations

    Every location that wants to be found in local search needs its own webpage with its own URL. All US Adriana Hoyos showrooms are currently listed together on a single shared page at adrianahoyos.com/locations/. This page cannot rank separately for each city, cannot be optimized for local keywords, and gets only one visit per month as a result. Competitors like Bo Concept have a dedicated page for each of their stores, and those pages rank for local searches individually.

    Technical detail: /locations/ page title reads "Showrooms | Adriana Hoyos" with no city specificity. Zero DC-related keyword rankings.

  • Backlink authority has been declining for 12 months

    The number of external websites linking to adrianahoyos.com dropped from approximately 900 to 657 over the past year, a decline of roughly 27%. Despite prominent press features in publications like Architectural Digest, Elle Decor, and Robb Report, almost all of those links are marked "nofollow," meaning they pass no ranking benefit to the site. The domain authority score has been flat at 27 out of 100 for the entire year.

    Technical detail: Authority Score 27 (SEMrush). Referring domains down from ~900 to 657. Majority of high-authority links are nofollow.

06 — Content and Access

The Current Structure Prevents Local Marketing from Happening

The technical limitations of the website are compounded by the operational structure governing US content. Based on the brand onboarding documentation and team call conducted in May 2026, the following constraints currently apply to any US location operating under the adrianahoyos.com domain.

Requirement Current Reality What Is Needed for Effective Local Marketing
Content publishing timeline 48-hour minimum approval from HQ Same-day or next-day capability for local events, promotions, news
Pricing and offers No pricing, no discounts in any content Ability to reference pricing ranges for local advertising context
Showroom-specific page No dedicated URL for any US showroom Individual location page with address, hours, events, local content
Local content creation Only approved brand assets permitted DC-specific photography, blog posts, design event coverage
Website access No direct access for US team Ability to update location-specific information independently
Google Business Profile Not confirmed as actively managed for DC Verified, optimized GBP with regular posts and updated hours

Digital marketing for a local showroom requires speed. When a customer searches for a store near them, looks up hours before visiting, or finds a design event through Google, the information needs to be current and the page needs to exist. Under the current structure, the Washington DC showroom has no mechanism to respond to local market opportunities online, regardless of how good the brand content is or how much effort the local team puts in. This is not a criticism of headquarters. It is a structural gap that the brand's growth in the US market depends on solving.

The Same Limitations Apply Directly to Paid Advertising

Paid search campaigns depend on the website in ways that make the access constraints above a direct cost issue, not just an operational inconvenience.

  • Ads send paid traffic to a page that cannot convert it

    Every paid ad needs a relevant landing page. Without a dedicated DC location page, any DC-targeted ad points visitors to the generic homepage. A visitor who clicks an ad for a DC showroom and lands on a brand homepage with no DC-specific information is unlikely to take the next step. That click cost money and produced nothing.

  • Google charges more when the landing page is irrelevant

    Google Ads assigns a Quality Score to every keyword based in part on how relevant the landing page is to the ad and the search. A DC-targeted ad pointing to a generic homepage receives a low landing page score, which raises the cost per click and reduces how often the ad is shown. Competitors with dedicated location pages pay less for the same clicks.

  • Conversion tracking cannot be installed without site access

    To measure whether paid ads are working, a tracking tag needs to be placed on the website. Without direct access, the DC team cannot install Google Ads conversion tracking. This means ad spend cannot be tied to outcomes, and campaigns cannot be optimized based on what is actually driving showroom visits or inquiries.

  • Time-sensitive campaigns cannot be supported

    Paid ads for a showroom opening, a new collection launch, or a local design event need a matching landing page that can go live at the same time as the ad. The 48-hour content approval window makes it structurally impossible to run responsive paid campaigns tied to real-world moments.

07 — Recommendations

Three Paths to a Stronger US Digital Presence

The goal of each option below is the same: make Adriana Hoyos findable by US customers who are actively searching for what the brand offers. Each path is scaled differently in terms of scope, investment, and HQ involvement. All three can coexist with and support the main adrianahoyos.com brand site.

01
Minimum Ask

Dedicated Location Pages for US Showrooms

Create an individual webpage for each US showroom within the existing adrianahoyos.com site. This is the same approach Bo Concept uses for every one of its locations.

  • URL structure: adrianahoyos.com/locations/washington-dc/
  • Unique page per city with local address, hours, team, and events
  • Local schema data so Google can read the location properly
  • Enables local search rankings for each city independently
  • HQ maintains full brand oversight and approval
02
Recommended

US Market Subfolder with Expedited Content Workflow

Build a US-specific section within adrianahoyos.com where the US team can publish local content with a faster approval process, while HQ retains oversight of all brand standards.

  • URL structure: adrianahoyos.com/us/ as a US market hub
  • Individual location pages for each US showroom
  • US blog, events, and design inspiration content
  • Same-day or next-day publishing for location-specific posts
  • Builds on existing domain authority
  • US team manages content, HQ approves brand-level material
03
Highest Potential

Dedicated US Market Website

Launch adrianahoyos.us as a fully independent US-facing website that operates alongside the main brand site and links directly back to it.

  • adrianahoyos.us — dedicated US market domain
  • Full local SEO capability from day one
  • US team has direct publishing access for local content
  • Location pages, product focus content, and US design blog
  • Follows the international market model used by other luxury brands
  • Brand standards and visual identity remain fully consistent

The minimum step the DC showroom needs before opening: a dedicated location page at adrianahoyos.com/locations/washington-dc/ with the showroom address, phone number, hours, and a brief description of the DC team. This single change would allow Google to register the DC showroom as a real location, enable local map pack rankings, and give the showroom a URL to use in all marketing materials. Without it, every dollar spent on offline marketing sends customers to a Google search that returns no results for the brand.

The Ask for This Conversation

Partnership, Not Independence

Nothing in this document is a request to operate outside brand guidelines or reduce headquarters' role in protecting the Adriana Hoyos name. Every option presented keeps HQ in control of brand identity, visual standards, and product messaging.

What we are asking for is the infrastructure to be discoverable in the cities where Adriana Hoyos has showrooms. The brand has the reputation, the products, and the design legacy. The gap is purely digital visibility at the local level, and it is a gap that every comparable competitor has already closed.
